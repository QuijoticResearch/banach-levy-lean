2.1 Truncated Measures

Definition 15 Truncated stable measure
✓
#

Lean declarations

\(\nu _\gamma ^M := \nu _\gamma |_{\{ |z|\leq M\} }\), the restriction to \(\{ |z| \leq M\} \). This finite measure enables compound Poisson approximation: the Itô formula reduces to a finite telescoping sum, and the full integral is recovered as \(M \to \infty \).

Theorem 16 Truncated second moment positive
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 15

Lean declarations

  • TruncatedStableMeasure.second_moment_pos

\(\int _{|z|\leq M} z^2\, \nu _\gamma (dz) = 2c_\gamma M^{2-\gamma }/(2-\gamma ) {\gt} 0\), since \(c_\gamma {\gt} 0\) and \(2 - \gamma {\gt} 0\). Provides the nondegenerate isometry constant for the truncated compensated integral.

2.2 Finite Telescoping

Theorem 17 Finite telescope
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • finite_telescope

\(f(y_n) - f(y_0) = \sum _{i=0}^{n-1} [f(y_{i+1}) - f(y_i)]\), proved via Finset.sum_range_sub. This replaces stochastic integration in the compound Poisson setting, where finitely many jumps make the Itô formula a finite sum.

Theorem 18 Jump linear plus remainder
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • jump_linear_plus_remainder

\(f(v+z) - f(v) = f'(v)z + [f(v+z) - f(v) - f'(v)z]\): the linear part (odd in \(z\), detectable by \(D_L\)) plus the nonlinear remainder (contributing to \(\Gamma _L\)).

Theorem 19 Product telescope
✓
#

Uses

  • Theorem 17

Lean declarations

  • product_telescope

Telescoping for \(h = ab\) gives the compound Poisson product rule: a Leibniz part plus a defect \([a(y_{i+1}) - a(y_i)][b(y_{i+1}) - b(y_i)]\). This is the finite-dimensional prototype of the Leibniz defect formula.
