3 Banach Energy Space Framework

3.1 Abstract Framework

Definition 44 Banach energy space
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • BanachEnergySpace

A Banach integrand space \(H_X\) with dual \(H_X^*\), \(L^p\)–\(L^q\) function spaces, divergence \(\delta : H_X \to L^p\), duality pairings, and expectation. Unlike the Hilbert framework, no Riesz identification \(H_X^* \cong H_X\) is assumed.

3.2 The Derivative \(D\)

Definition 47 Operator-covariant derivative
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 44

Lean declarations

  • BanachEnergySpace.D

Constructed as \(D(F) := \mathrm{mk\_ dual}(u \mapsto \mathbb {E}[F \cdot \delta (u)])\). The map \(u \mapsto \mathbb {E}[F \cdot \delta (u)]\) is bounded linear on \(H_X\) by Hölder, so \(D(F) \in H_X^*\). The defining identity \(\langle DF, u\rangle = \mathbb {E}[F \cdot \delta (u)]\) is a theorem, not a definition.

3.4 Representability Obstruction

✓

Uses

\(L \stackrel{d}{=} -L\): the negation map preserves even functionals (depending on \(|\Delta L_s|\)) while flipping the sign of \(\delta _L(u)\). This parity mismatch drives the even annihilation lemma.

Theorem 55 Even annihilates \(\mathrm{im}(\delta )\)
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 54
  • Theorem 48

Lean declarations

  • BanachEnergySpace.even_annihilates_im_delta

If \(G\) is even, then \(\mathbb {E}[G \cdot \delta (u)] = 0\) for all \(u\): under \(L \mapsto -L\), \(G\) is invariant while \(\delta (u)\) flips sign, forcing \(\mathbb {E}[G \cdot \delta (u)] = -\mathbb {E}[G \cdot \delta (u)] = 0\).

Theorem 56 Representability obstruction
✓
#

Uses

  • Theorem 55
  • Theorem 46

Lean declarations

  • BanachEnergySpace.representability_obstruction

Proposition 5.1. Centered, even, nonzero \(G \Rightarrow G \notin \mathrm{im}(\delta _L)\). By contradiction: \(G = \delta (u)\) gives \(\| G\| _2^2 = \mathbb {E}[G \cdot \delta (u)] = 0\) by even annihilation, so \(G = 0\). This proves \(\mathrm{im}(\delta _L) \subsetneq L^p_0(\Omega )\).

3.5 Leibniz Defect (Theorem B)

Definition 57 Leibniz defect
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 47

Lean declarations

  • BanachEnergySpace.leibniz_defect

\(\Gamma (F,G) := D(FG) - F \cdot DG - G \cdot DF \in H_X^*\): measures failure of the product rule. \(\Gamma = 0\) in the Hilbert (Brownian) case; \(\Gamma \neq 0\) captures the jump correction for stable processes.

Theorem 58 Product rule with jump defect
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 57

Lean declarations

  • BanachEnergySpace.product_rule_with_jump_defect

Theorem B. \(\langle \Gamma (F,G), u \rangle = \mathbb {E}[\sum _s \Delta _s(f,g) \cdot \delta (u)]\), where \(\Delta _s(f,g)\) is the product jump defect. The proof applies the Lévy–Itô formula to \(fg\) and identifies the non-Leibniz part with the jump residual.

Theorem 59 Defect vanishes without jumps
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 57

Lean declarations

  • BanachEnergySpace.defect_vanishes_without_jumps

No jumps \(\Rightarrow \) \(\Gamma = 0\): the classical product rule holds for continuous processes. The Leibniz defect is a pure jump phenomenon.

Theorem 60 Chaos kernel characterization
✓
#

Uses

  • Theorem 48

Lean declarations
