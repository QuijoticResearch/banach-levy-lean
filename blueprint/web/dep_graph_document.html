Definition 44Banach energy space

A Banach integrand space \(H_X\) with dual \(H_X^*\), \(L^p\)–\(L^q\) function spaces, divergence \(\delta : H_X \to L^p\), duality pairings, and expectation. Unlike the Hilbert framework, no Riesz identification \(H_X^* \cong H_X\) is assumed, which is precisely the generalization required by the singular Lévy measure.

Definition 1Stable Lévy density

The symmetric \(\gamma \)-stable Lévy measure density is \(\nu _\gamma (dz) = c_\gamma |z|^{-1-\gamma }\, dz\) on \(\mathbb {R} \setminus \{ 0\} \), where \(c_\gamma {\gt} 0\) and \(\gamma \in (1,2)\). The singularity at the origin is strong enough that \(\int |z|^2\, \nu _\gamma (dz) = \infty \), forcing the energy space to be Banach rather than Hilbert. This density is the starting point for the entire Lévy–Itô construction and all moment estimates that follow; see [4, 5] for background on stable processes.
